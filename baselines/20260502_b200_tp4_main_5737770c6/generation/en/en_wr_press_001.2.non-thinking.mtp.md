**HARBORLEAF, CA – July 1, 2026** – The Harborleaf Botanical Garden announced today the opening of its newest public education amenity: the “Pollinator Path,” a dedicated trail designed to teach visitors how small, intentional planting choices can support bees, butterflies, moths, and hummingbirds. The trail officially opens to the public on July 12, 2026, at 9:00 a.m.

Located along the east meadow loop, the 0.8-mile accessible trail offers a self-guided, immersive learning experience for visitors of all ages and abilities. The Pollinator Path was developed in response to a growing number of visitor inquiries seeking practical guidance on supporting local pollinators in home gardens and community green spaces.

The new trail features a series of native plant demonstration beds, each showcasing species that provide essential nectar, pollen, and host resources for specific pollinator groups. A dedicated night-blooming flower station highlights plants that open at dusk to attract moths and nocturnal pollinators, offering a unique learning opportunity during evening garden hours. Throughout the route, tactile signage allows visitors with visual impairments to engage with the content through raised illustrations and braille descriptions, while shaded resting points provide comfort and encourage longer stays for observation.

Youth observation cards are available at the trailhead, designed to help children and families document the species they encounter. The cards encourage active participation and can be used to track sightings over multiple visits. In addition, a volunteer-led weekend walk will begin the weekend following the opening, offering guided interpretation along the trail.

Maya Ortiz, Director of Learning Programs at Harborleaf Botanical Garden, emphasized that the Pollinator Path is rooted in the belief that individual actions, when multiplied, can create meaningful ecological change.

We want to show that even a single pot of native milkweed or a small patch of wild bergamot can serve as a critical stepping stone for a monarch or a bumblebee. The Pollinator Path is free with general garden admission. Harborleaf Botanical Garden is open daily from 8:00 a.m. to sunset. The volunteer-led weekend walk begins July 18, 2026, and will be offered every Saturday and Sunday morning at 10:00 a.m. through October.

**About Harborleaf Botanical Garden**

Harborleaf Botanical Garden is a public botanical garden dedicated to plant conservation, environmental education, and community connection. Harborleaf’s learning programs serve over 30,000 youth and adults annually through workshops, school field trips, and public events.
