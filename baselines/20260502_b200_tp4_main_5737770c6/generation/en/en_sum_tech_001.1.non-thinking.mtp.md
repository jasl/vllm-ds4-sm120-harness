### Key Terms (with One-Sentence Definitions)
1. **Water cycle**: The continuous movement of water among Earth’s storage pools, driven by solar energy and gravity.
2. **Evaporation**: The process by which liquid water changes into water vapor and enters the atmosphere.
3. **Evapotranspiration**: The combined movement of water into the atmosphere from land surfaces and oceans, including plant transpiration.
4. **Precipitation**: Water falling from clouds as rain, snow, or hail due to gravity.
5. **Runoff**: Water that flows across the land surface into rivers, lakes, or oceans.
6. **Infiltration**: The movement of water from the land surface into the ground.
7. **Groundwater recharge**: The process by which infiltrated water moves downward to replenish underground aquifers.
8. **Groundwater discharge**: The natural return of groundwater to the surface through springs, rivers, or the ocean.

### Human Influence
Human actions impact water movement, storage, and availability through water use, land use, and climate change. Water use (e.g., extraction for drinking, agriculture, industry) can reduce water stored in pools like rivers, lakes, and aquifers. Land use (e.g., urbanization, deforestation) alters runoff, infiltration, and evapotranspiration rates. Climate change affects the timing and intensity of precipitation, evaporation, and snowmelt, thereby modifying how fast and where water moves through the cycle. These changes influence how much water is available in each pool and its cleanliness.
