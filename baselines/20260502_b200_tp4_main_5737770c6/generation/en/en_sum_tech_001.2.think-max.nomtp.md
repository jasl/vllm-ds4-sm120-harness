### Key Terms (with Definitions)
1. **Water cycle**: The continuous movement of water among Earth’s storage pools (atmosphere, land, oceans, underground).
2. **Evaporation**: The process by which solar energy turns liquid water into water vapor.
3. **Evapotranspiration**: The main way water moves into the atmosphere from land surfaces and oceans.
4. **Precipitation**: Rain, snow, or hail that falls from clouds due to gravity.
5. **Infiltration**: The movement of water from the land surface into the ground.
6. **Groundwater recharge**: The process by which infiltrating water replenishes underground aquifers.
7. **Runoff**: Water that flows across the land surface toward streams, rivers, or oceans.
8. **Aquifer**: An underground layer that stores and transmits groundwater.

### Human Influence
Human actions affect the water cycle through water use, land use, and climate change. Water withdrawal for consumption, industry, and agriculture alters storage in rivers, lakes, and aquifers. Land-use changes (e.g., urbanization, deforestation) modify infiltration, runoff, and evapotranspiration rates. Climate change influences precipitation patterns, snowpack duration, and evaporation rates, thereby affecting water movement, storage timing, and overall availability.
