Silas was a careful snail. He never slid into a puddle he hadn’t mapped. He knew which ones were deep enough to swim in and which were only a sip. Today, after the rain, he had to update his map. He was about to measure the Big Blue with his stretching neck when a *whirr* and a *thump* landed beside him.

“That’s very high,” said Silas, blinking his stalk eyes.

“It’s the best! The wind pushed me up like a giant breath! I want to fly all the way to the roof next. Or the clouds! Have you ever been to a cloud?”

Pip hopped in a circle. “You take so long! I want to go *fast*. Watch this!”

“I *am* brave! I flew straight into the wind!”

Silas smiled as much as a snail can smile. “You saw the puddle first. You knew where to put your feet.”
