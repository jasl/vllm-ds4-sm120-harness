### Key Terms (at least eight)
1. **Evaporation** – Process by which liquid water turns into water vapor due to solar energy.
2. **Evapotranspiration** – Combined movement of water into the atmosphere from land and oceans via evaporation and plant transpiration.
3. **Precipitation** – Water falling from clouds as rain, snow, or hail, driven by gravity.
4. **Runoff** – Water flowing across the land surface toward streams, rivers, or oceans.
5. **Infiltration** – Movement of water from the land surface into the ground.
6. **Groundwater recharge** – Process by which infiltrated water enters and replenishes underground aquifers.
7. **Aquifer** – Underground layer of rock or sediment that stores and transmits groundwater.
8. **Watershed** – Large-scale area where water drains across the land surface into a common outlet.

### Human Influence
Human actions impact water movement, storage, and availability through water use and land use. Water use (e.g., withdrawals for drinking, agriculture, industry) alters the amount and timing of water in surface and groundwater pools. Land-use changes (e.g., construction, deforestation, agriculture) modify infiltration, runoff, and evapotranspiration rates. These effects can change how water moves between pools, how much is stored in each pool, and the cleanliness of available water. Understanding these influences supports efforts to use water sustainably.
