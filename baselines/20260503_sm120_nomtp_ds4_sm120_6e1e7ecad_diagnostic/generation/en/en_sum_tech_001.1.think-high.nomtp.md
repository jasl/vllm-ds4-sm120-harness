### Key Terms
- **Water cycle**: The continuous movement and storage of water on Earth.
- **Evaporation**: The process by which liquid water changes into water vapor due to solar energy.
- **Evapotranspiration**: The main way water moves into the atmosphere from land surfaces and oceans.
- **Precipitation**: Rain, snow, or hail that falls from clouds due to gravity.
- **Runoff**: Water that moves across the land surface toward streams or oceans.
- **Infiltration**: The movement of water from the surface into the ground.
- **Groundwater recharge**: The process by which water enters underground aquifers.
- **Aquifer**: An underground layer where groundwater flows and is stored.

### Human influence
Human actions affect the water cycle through water use, land use, and climate change. Water use (e.g., withdrawals for agriculture, industry, or households) alters the amount of water in pools and movement rates. Land use changes, such as urban development or deforestation, modify infiltration, runoff, and evapotranspiration. Climate change influences evaporation, precipitation patterns, and snowmelt timing. These impacts collectively change water storage, movement speeds, and water cleanliness, thereby affecting water availability for organisms.
