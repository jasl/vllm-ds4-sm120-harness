Noa was a careful snail. You could even call him a *professional* careful snail. While other snails slid around without a plan, Noa had a system. He carried his home on his back, of course, but he also carried something else: a rolled-up leaf he called his Map of Puddles.

He crawled slowly toward Puddle Four, his slimy trail shining like a silver thread. Every few minutes, he stopped and made a tiny mark on his map. A dry patch of sun could be dangerous. A forgotten crack could be a trap. But puddles? Puddles were perfect drinking spots and places to cool his foot.
