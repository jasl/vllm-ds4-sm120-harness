Like galloping horses, like drifting dust, all living things blow on one another with their breath. The sky’s deep blue—is it the true color? Or is it so far that there is no end? When the Peng looks down from above, it must seem the same.

If water is not deep enough, it cannot carry a big boat. Spill a cup of water in a hollow on the floor—a mustard seed becomes a boat. But put a cup there, and it sticks—water is too shallow, and the boat is too big. If wind is not thick enough, it cannot carry big wings. So at ninety thousand miles high, the wind is below it, and only then can it ride the wind; with the blue sky on its back and nothing in the way, only then can it head south.
