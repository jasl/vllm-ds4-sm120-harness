“Tomorrow,” she said, “will you show me the whole map? I could fly from puddle to puddle. I will land perfectly on every single one.”

“I would like that very much,” Silas said.

And as the very last drop of Puddle 7 vanished into the quiet evening air, Pippa did not feel like flying anymore. She just felt the quiet bravery of being perfectly still—a perfectly landed moment on the edge of the world.
