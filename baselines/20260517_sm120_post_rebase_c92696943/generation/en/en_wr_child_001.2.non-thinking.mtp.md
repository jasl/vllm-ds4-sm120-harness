Silas was not a fast snail. He was a *careful* snail. Each morning he made a new map. He drew it with a trail of shiny slime on a flat, gray stone. His map was not of faraway lands or secret treasures. His map was of *puddles*.

Using a tiny twig, he traced a wobbly circle. “The Big One,” he whispered to himself. “Beside the rosemary bush. Very deep. For a bath.”

Suddenly, a blur of feathers and noise landed on a sunflower stalk right above him.

“BOO!” cheeped the blur.

Silas pulled his head into his shell. He waited three slow breaths before peeking out. A young sparrow, no bigger than his shell, was bobbing up and down on the stalk. Her name was Pip.

“You’re so slow, Silas!” chirped Pip. “All the best puddles are evaporating!”
