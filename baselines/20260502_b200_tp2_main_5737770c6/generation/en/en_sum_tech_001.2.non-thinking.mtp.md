### Key Terms (with Definitions from Passage)
1. **Evaporation** – The process by which solar energy causes liquid water to change into water vapor.
2. **Evapotranspiration** – The main way water moves into the atmosphere from the land surface and oceans.
3. **Precipitation** – Rain, snow, or hail that falls from clouds due to gravity.
4. **Runoff** – Water that moves across the land surface toward rivers, lakes, or oceans.
5. **Infiltration** – The movement of water from the land surface into the ground.
6. **Groundwater** – Water stored underground within aquifers.
7. **Groundwater recharge** – The process by which infiltrated water replenishes underground water storage.
8. **Aquifer** – An underground layer that holds and transmits groundwater.
9. **Watershed** – A large-scale land area where water moves across and drains to a common outlet.
10. **Saline water** – Water that is salty, as found in oceans and saline lakes.

### Human Influence
Human water use, land use, and climate change affect the water cycle by altering how water moves, where it is stored, and how much is available. For example, withdrawals from lakes, rivers, and groundwater can reduce pool volumes. Land use changes—such as paving surfaces—can decrease infiltration and increase runoff. These actions change the timing and speed of water movement through the cycle, impacting water availability and cleanliness for humans and other organisms.
