### Key Terms (with Definitions)
1. **Water cycle** – The continuous movement and storage of water on Earth, including changes between liquid, solid, and gas.
2. **Evaporation** – The process by which liquid water turns into water vapor, driven by solar energy.
3. **Evapotranspiration** – The combined movement of water from land and oceans into the atmosphere through evaporation and plant transpiration.
4. **Precipitation** – Water falling from clouds as rain, snow, or hail, driven by gravity.
5. **Infiltration** – The movement of water from the land surface into the ground.
6. **Groundwater recharge** – The process by which infiltrated water replenishes underground aquifers.
7. **Runoff** – Water flowing across the land surface, typically toward streams, rivers, or oceans.
8. **Aquifer** – An underground layer that stores and transmits groundwater.

### Human Influence
Human actions affect water movement, storage, and availability through water use and land use changes. These activities alter how water cycles between pools, influencing the timing and speed of flows such as runoff, infiltration, and evaporation. Human water consumption reduces the amount available in certain pools, while land-use modifications (e.g., construction, agriculture) can change infiltration rates and surface runoff patterns. Understanding these impacts is essential for sustainable water management.
