The Keeper of the Puddle Map

It wasn't a map of countries or roads. It was a map of puddles. Silas had drawn it himself, using a blade of grass as a pencil and a flat stone as his desk. Each puddle had a name. Silas pushed his shell forward, his body a slow, sleek ribbon over the wet earth. He found Spoonful Sea and traced it on his map with his foot. *Still full,* he wrote. He slid to the Long Lake. *A little smaller, but deep.*

“Oof!” said the blur.

It was a young sparrow. She was small and round, with a beak that looked too new for her face. Her name was Pip.

“Are you all right?” asked Silas.

“Fine!” chirped Pip, shaking dirt from her wing. “I was just… testing the air.”

“You were falling,” said Silas gently.

“I was *arriving* quickly,” said Pip. She hopped up to a low branch, fluffed her feathers, and glared at the sky. “I want to fly *up*. High up. I want to see over the fence.”

“Can you fly up?” asked Silas.

“I don’t know,” Pip admitted. “I can go down very well. Down is easy. But every time I try to go up, I get dizzy, and then I come down again. Fast.”

Silas nodded. He knew something about speed. His greatest adventure was racing a lazy earthworm once. The earthworm had won.
