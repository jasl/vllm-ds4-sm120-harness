### Overview
The water cycle describes the continuous movement of water on Earth, stored in oceans, atmosphere, land surface, and underground. It changes form between liquid, solid, and gas. Energy from the sun and gravity drive processes like evaporation, precipitation, runoff, and infiltration. Humans affect the cycle through water use and land use, influencing water storage, movement, and availability. Understanding these impacts supports sustainable water use.

### Concept Map (Water Movement Among Major Pools)
Atmosphere → (Precipitation) → Land surface & Oceans
Land surface → (Evapotranspiration) → Atmosphere
Land surface → (Runoff, Streamflow) → Oceans
Land surface → (Infiltration, Recharge) → Groundwater
Groundwater → (Discharge to rivers, oceans, springs) → Land surface & Oceans
Oceans → (Evaporation) → Atmosphere
Snowpack/Ice → (Snowmelt) → Land surface & Streamflow

### Key Terms (with Definitions)
1. **Evaporation** – Process by which liquid water turns into water vapor, driven by the sun’s energy.
2. **Precipitation** – Water falling from clouds as rain, snow, or hail, driven by gravity.
3. **Evapotranspiration** – The main way water moves into the atmosphere from land surfaces and oceans.
4. **Runoff** – Water moving across the land surface toward streams or oceans.
5. **Infiltration** – Water moving into the ground from the surface.
6. **Groundwater recharge** – The process by which infiltrated water replenishes underground aquifers.
7. **Aquifer** – An underground layer where groundwater flows and is stored.
8. **Groundwater discharge** – Natural return of groundwater to rivers, oceans, or springs.

### Human Influence
Human water use and land use alter the natural movement, storage, and availability of water. These actions can change how much water is stored in pools (e.g., reservoirs), how fast it moves (e.g., via irrigation or drainage), and its cleanliness. Understanding these human impacts helps guide sustainable water management.
