### Key Terms and One-Sentence Definitions
1. **Evaporation**: Process where solar energy turns liquid water into water vapor.
2. **Evapotranspiration**: Main way water moves into the atmosphere from land and oceans, combining evaporation and plant transpiration.
3. **Precipitation**: Rain, snow, or hail falling from clouds due to gravity.
4. **Runoff**: Water flowing across the land surface toward streams and oceans.
5. **Infiltration**: Water moving from the land surface into the ground.
6. **Groundwater recharge**: Process by which infiltrated water replenishes underground aquifers.
7. **Aquifer**: Underground rock or sediment layer that stores and transmits groundwater.
8. **Watershed**: Large-scale area where water drains across the land surface into a common outlet.

### Human Influence
Human water use and land use affect water movement, storage, and availability. Water use directly removes water from pools, altering storage volumes. Land use changes (e.g., construction, agriculture) modify infiltration, runoff, and evapotranspiration rates, impacting how fast water moves through the cycle. These actions also influence water cleanliness, which affects the amount of usable water in each pool.
