Write an 800–1,000 word story for children ages 7–10. The main characters are a careful snail who keeps a map of every puddle in the garden and a young sparrow who wants to fly before she has learned how to land. The story should take place after a summer rain.

Requirements:
- Complete story arc with a gentle problem and resolution.
- Warm, concrete language suitable for children.
- Include one simple nature concept, such as how puddles dry or how birds use wind, explained naturally through the story.
- Theme: patience can be a kind of bravery.
- No violence, death, horror, or adult romance.
